My first published model, now live under OpenLLM-Ro, the first open-source initiative for Romanian LLMs. It's a 4-bit, 1B Llama taught Romanian through a constrained continual-pretraining run, with both the model and the recipe built for efficient on-device inference.

The recipe is an optimized QLoRA run on Unsloth, where I tuned the levers of a constrained CPT run: sequence packing, LoRA rank, and data mix. The experiment I cared about most was whether higher educational-quality data makes a better model, and it did: language understanding improved across the board, while reasoning- and knowledge-heavy tasks gained the most from the filtered data.

Along the way I configured a custom training loop by subclassing parts of the Transformers library, and leaned on Nsight profiling as an investigative tool to work out why a packing experiment wasn't behaving the way I expected.

// what I learned

  • If you use the right tools, even continual pretraining a (small) model is feasible on consumer hardware, though the scale isn't comparable to what labs do (billions of tokens, not trillions).
  • Profiling training runs opens up a whole new world of optimizations, and helps provide empirical proof for why a hypothesis may or may not hold.
  • Setting up a Warm-Stable-Decay scheduler was a life-saver: being able to extend training mid-run saved a lot of time.
