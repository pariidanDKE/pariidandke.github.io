My first published model, now live under OpenLLM-Ro, the first open-source initiative for Romanian LLMs. It's a 4-bit, 1B Llama taught Romanian through a constrained continual-pretraining run, with both the model and the recipe built for efficient on-device inference.

The recipe is an optimized QLoRA run on Unsloth, where I went deep on the levers that actually move a small model: sequence packing, LoRA rank, and data mix. The experiment I cared about most was whether higher educational-quality data makes a better model, and it did: language understanding improved across the board, while reasoning- and knowledge-heavy tasks gained the most from the filtered data.

Getting there meant subclassing large parts of the Transformers library to shape the exact training loop I wanted, and profiling CUDA kernels with Nsight to keep it fast.

// what I learned

  • Data quality compounds: filtering for educational-quality data lifted language understanding everywhere, with the biggest gains on reasoning- and knowledge-heavy tasks.
  • The levers that move a small model are unglamorous: sequence packing, LoRA rank and data mix did more than any clever trick.
  • To get the exact training loop I wanted I had to subclass large parts of Transformers, and profile CUDA kernels with Nsight to keep it fast.
